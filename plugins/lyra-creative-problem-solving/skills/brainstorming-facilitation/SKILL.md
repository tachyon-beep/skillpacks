# Brainstorming Facilitation

## Overview

Effective brainstorming generates abundant, diverse ideas by creating the right environment and structure. Most brainstorms fail due to premature judgment, groupthink, and dominant voices—all preventable with proper facilitation.

**Core Principle**: Separate idea generation from evaluation. Generate quantity (50-100+ ideas) to find quality.

**Research insight**: Classic verbal brainstorming often underperforms individual work due to production blocking, evaluation apprehension, and social loafing. Proper facilitation overcomes these obstacles.

## When to Use

Use brainstorming facilitation when:
- Need to generate many solution ideas
- Running team ideation session
- Want diverse perspectives on problem
- Starting innovation workshops
- User mentions: "brainstorm", "ideate", "generate ideas"

**Don't use for**:
- Evaluating existing ideas (use idea-evaluation-frameworks)
- Developing detailed solutions (use prototyping-strategies)
- Individual thinking (different techniques more effective)

## The Four Rules (IDEO/d.school)

### 1. Defer Judgment

**No criticism during generation**. "Yes, but..." kills ideas.

❌ Bad facilitation:
- "That won't work because..."
- "We tried that already"
- "Too expensive"
- "Legal would never allow it"

✅ Good facilitation:
- "Capture it"
- "What else?"
- "Keep going"
- [Silence—let ideas flow]

**Why**: Judgment during generation stops creative flow. Even self-censoring ("that's stupid, won't say it") reduces output.

**Facilitator's job**: Enforce the rule. Interrupt judgment immediately.

### 2. Encourage Wild Ideas

**Unconventional, ambitious, weird ideas are welcome**.

The wilder the better because:
- Wild ideas spark other ideas
- Easier to tame wild idea than energize boring one
- Breakthrough solutions often seem crazy initially
- Permission to be wild frees conventional thinkers

**Facilitator prompts**:
- "What's the most ambitious version?"
- "What if resources were unlimited?"
- "What would [eccentric person/company] do?"

### 3. Build on Ideas of Others

**"Yes, and..." not "Yes, but..."**

Combinatorial creativity: Ideas combine to create better ideas.

❌ **Yes, but** (judgment):
- "Yes, but that's too expensive"
- "Yes, but users won't understand"

✅ **Yes, and** (building):
- "Yes, and we could simplify it by..."
- "Yes, and what if we combined it with..."

**Facilitator prompts**:
- "Who can build on that?"
- "How could we make that even better?"
- "What if we combined [idea A] with [idea B]?"

### 4. Stay Visual

**Sketch, diagram, show—don't just talk**.

Why visual:
- Communicates faster than words
- Clarifies fuzzy ideas
- Engages different thinking mode
- Creates shared understanding
- Easier to build on visual than verbal

**Visual tools**:
- Whiteboard sketches
- Sticky notes
- Simple diagrams
- Gesture/body language
- Physical props

## Brainwriting: The Secret Weapon

**Problem with verbal brainstorming**: People talk one at a time. If 8 people, 7 are idle.

**Solution**: Brainwriting (silent, written idea generation).

### Process

```
1. Individual silent generation (5-7 min)
   - Everyone writes ideas on sticky notes
   - One idea per note
   - Sketch if helpful
   - Aim for 10-20 ideas each

2. Share and cluster (10 min)
   - Everyone puts notes on wall
   - Read aloud as posting
   - Group similar ideas
   - No discussion yet, just clustering

3. Build collectively (10-15 min)
   - Identify interesting clusters
   - "What else in this direction?"
   - Build on each other's ideas
   - Add to clusters
```

**Why it works**:
- ✅ Everyone generates simultaneously (no production blocking)
- ✅ No dominant voices
- ✅ No evaluation apprehension (anonymous notes)
- ✅ Individual deep thinking first
- ✅ Then collective building

**Research**: Brainwriting typically generates 50-100% more ideas than verbal brainstorming.

## Session Structure

### Classic 60-Minute Brainstorm

```
0:00-0:05  Frame the challenge (5 min)
           - Present "How Might We" question
           - Show any relevant context/constraints
           - Answer clarifying questions only

0:05-0:15  Individual brainwriting (10 min)
           - Silent generation
           - Sticky notes, one idea each
           - Aim for 15-20 ideas per person

0:15-0:25  Share and cluster (10 min)
           - Post all ideas on wall
           - Read aloud while posting
           - Group similar concepts
           - No judgment, just organizing

0:25-0:40  Collective building (15 min)
           - Identify interesting directions
           - Build on clusters
           - Combine ideas
           - Push concepts further

0:40-0:55  Dot voting (15 min)
           - Each person gets 5 dots
           - Vote for most promising ideas
           - Can put multiple dots on one idea
           - Discuss top 5-10 vote-getters

0:55-1:00  Next steps (5 min)
           - Who will develop top ideas?
           - When to reconvene?
           - Document everything
```

### Quick 20-Minute Sprint

```
0:00-0:02  Frame challenge (2 min)
0:02-0:08  Individual brainwriting (6 min)
0:08-0:12  Share (round-robin, 30 sec each) (4 min)
0:12-0:18  Build together (6 min)
0:18-0:20  Quick dot vote, next steps (2 min)
```

### 2-Hour Workshop (Multiple Rounds)

```
0:00-0:15  Intro, context, framing
0:15-0:45  Round 1: Core HMW question
0:45-1:00  Break
1:00-1:30  Round 2: Different angle/technique
1:30-1:50  Synthesis and prioritization
1:50-2:00  Next steps
```

## Ideation Techniques

Mix techniques to generate diverse ideas:

### 1. Classic Brainstorming
Individual brainwriting → Share → Build (described above)

### 2. Worst Possible Idea
**Prompt**: "How might we make this problem worse?"

**Why it works**:
- Hilarious, relaxing, breaks tension
- Removes performance pressure
- Often reveals inverse solutions
- Identifies risks to avoid

**Example**:
- Problem: Improve customer onboarding
- Worst ideas: Make it 50 steps, use jargon, never give help, crash randomly
- Inverted: Simple flow, plain language, proactive help, reliability

### 3. Bodystorming
**Act out** the experience physically.

**Process**:
- Assign roles (user, system, environment)
- Physically walk through scenario
- Improvise solutions on the spot

**Best for**: Service design, physical products, user journeys

### 4. Sketching Sprint
**Everyone draws** their idea (even "non-artists").

**Process**:
- 5 minutes: Sketch your idea
- Simple boxes, arrows, stick figures
- Label key parts
- Share sketches (1 min each)

**Why it works**: Sketching clarifies fuzzy ideas, creates shared vision

### 5. "Yes, and..." Circle
**Build on previous idea** in circle.

**Process**:
- First person: States idea
- Next person: "Yes, and we could also..."
- Continue around circle
- Each person builds on accumulated idea

**Best for**: Developing one direction deeply

### 6. Analogies
**Cross-domain inspiration**.

**Prompts**:
- "How would [Disney/Apple/SpaceX] solve this?"
- "What does nature's solution look like?"
- "How do other industries handle similar problems?"

**Example**: "How would a video game handle user onboarding?" → Tutorial levels, progressive complexity, rewards, safe practice

## Facilitator Techniques

### Energy Management

**High energy needed**: Use stand-up sessions, movement, music, props

**Signs of low energy**:
- Silence (not productive thinking kind)
- Phone checking
- Side conversations
- Repetitive ideas

**Energy boosters**:
- Change technique
- Take break
- Switch to physical activity (bodystorming)
- Play energizing music
- Competition (most ideas in 5 min)

### Handling Challenges

**Dominant Voices**:
- ❌ "Everyone gets a turn" (awkward, slow)
- ✅ Brainwriting (naturally equalizes)
- ✅ "Let's hear from people who haven't shared yet"

**Premature Judgment**:
- ❌ Debate why idea won't work
- ✅ Interrupt: "Capture the idea, we'll evaluate later"
- ✅ Remind: "Remember, defer judgment"

**Stuck/Repetitive Ideas**:
- ✅ Switch technique
- ✅ New prompt: "What if [constraint] didn't exist?"
- ✅ Random word injection
- ✅ Take break

**Tangents**:
- ✅ "Interesting, but let's stay focused on [HMW question]"
- ✅ Parking lot for off-topic ideas

**Silence**:
- ✅ Comfortable silence = thinking (allow it)
- ✅ Uncomfortable silence = stuck (intervene with new prompt)

## Remote Brainstorming

### Tools

**Digital whiteboards**: Miro, Mural, FigJam
**Features needed**:
- Sticky notes
- Clustering/grouping
- Voting
- Timer
- Templates

### Remote-Specific Adaptations

**Individual brainwriting**:
- Everyone adds sticky notes simultaneously
- Set timer (visible to all)
- No talking during generation

**Share and cluster**:
- Breakout rooms (groups of 3-4)
- Small groups cluster their ideas
- Return to main room, each group presents clusters

**Collective building**:
- Facilitator calls attention to interesting cluster
- Open floor for additions
- Build in real-time

**Challenges**:
- Harder to read energy (rely on polls, reactions)
- Tech friction (have backup plan)
- Zoom fatigue (shorter sessions, more breaks)

## Quantity Targets

**Why quantity matters**: Quality correlates with quantity. First 20 ideas are obvious. Ideas 40-60 get interesting.

**Targets by session length**:
- 20-min sprint: 30-50 ideas
- 60-min session: 80-120 ideas
- 2-hour workshop: 150-250 ideas

**Per person**:
- 7-min brainwriting: 10-15 ideas
- 15-min brainwriting: 20-30 ideas

**Falling short?**
- Not enough time for individual generation
- Judgment happening (shuts down flow)
- Prompt too narrow
- Need wilder prompting

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Skipping Individual Time
**Symptom**: Go straight to group discussion
**Why bad**: Groupthink, dominant voices, production blocking
**Fix**: Always start with individual brainwriting

### ❌ Evaluating During Generation
**Symptom**: "That won't work" during brainstorm
**Why bad**: Kills creative flow, signals judgment
**Fix**: Strictly enforce "defer judgment" rule

### ❌ Too Little Time
**Symptom**: 10-minute brainstorm, expect 50 ideas
**Why bad**: Barely past obvious ideas
**Fix**: Minimum 30 minutes for real ideation

### ❌ One Technique Only
**Symptom**: Verbal round-robin, nothing else
**Why bad**: Misses other idea-generation modes
**Fix**: Mix techniques (brainwriting, sketching, provocation)

### ❌ No Follow-Up
**Symptom**: Generate 100 ideas, never revisit
**Why bad**: Wasted effort, breeds cynicism
**Fix**: Always have evaluation and next steps

---

## Evaluation After Generation

**Separate session** (or separate phase). NEVER during generation.

**Quick prioritization**:
- Dot voting (each person 5 dots)
- Top 10 vote-getters

**Deeper evaluation**:
- Use idea-evaluation-frameworks skill
- 2x2 matrix (impact vs effort)
- Feasibility, desirability, viability

## Real-World Examples

### IDEO Shopping Cart Redesign
**Method**: Interdisciplinary team, multiple ideation rounds
**Output**: 100s of ideas → clustered themes → 5 prototypes
**Techniques**: Bodystorming, sketching, building on ideas
**Result**: Innovative cart design (modularity, child safety, scanner holder)

### Pixar Brain Trust
**Method**: Regular brainstorming on film problems
**Key rule**: Candor without judgment of person
**Output**: Solutions to story/character challenges
**Culture**: Safe to throw wild ideas, build collectively

### Google Sprint (Jake Knapp)
**Day 2 = Sketch day**: Individual idea generation
**Method**: Everyone sketches solutions (detailed, 4-panel)
**Why**: Avoids groupthink, surfaces diverse approaches
**Next**: Vote on sketches, prototype winner

## Quick Reference

### Pre-Session Prep
- [ ] Clear "How Might We" question
- [ ] Materials ready (sticky notes, markers, wall space)
- [ ] Diverse participants (4-8 people ideal)
- [ ] Room/digital space prepared
- [ ] 30-60 min blocked, no interruptions

### During Session
- [ ] Explain four rules (defer judgment, wild ideas, build on, visual)
- [ ] Individual brainwriting first (minimum 5 min)
- [ ] Share without judgment
- [ ] Cluster similar ideas
- [ ] Build collectively
- [ ] Enforce "defer judgment" strictly

### After Session
- [ ] Document all ideas (photos, digital archive)
- [ ] Schedule evaluation session (separate from generation)
- [ ] Assign owners to develop top ideas
- [ ] Thank participants, share output

## The Bottom Line

Effective brainstorming:
1. **Individual brainwriting first** (prevents groupthink)
2. **Defer judgment** (strictly enforced)
3. **Generate quantity** (50-100+ ideas minimum)
4. **Mix techniques** (brainwriting, worst idea, sketching, analogies)
5. **Evaluate later** (separate session)

**Most common failure**: Skipping individual time, allowing judgment during generation.

**Success metric**: Did we generate 50+ ideas? If not, something went wrong.
