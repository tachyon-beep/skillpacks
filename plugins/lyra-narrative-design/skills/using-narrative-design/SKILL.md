# Using Narrative Design

## Overview

**Lyra's narrative design skills transform technical artifacts into compelling stories.** Every README, changelog, demo, or presentation is an opportunity to engage, inform, and inspire your audience.

This router skill helps you select the right narrative design skill for your current task.

## When to Use Narrative Design Skills

**Use these skills when:**
- Creating any user-facing documentation
- Preparing presentations or talks
- Writing about technical work for broad audiences
- Communicating project value to stakeholders
- Building your professional presence
- Launching new features or products

**Narrative design matters because:**
- First impressions determine whether users engage with your project
- Stories make complex technical concepts accessible
- Emotional resonance drives adoption and advocacy
- Clear narratives reduce cognitive load
- Compelling communication amplifies technical excellence

## Core Narrative Principles

All Lyra narrative design skills build on these foundations:

### 1. Know Your Audience
- **Developers**: Show code, emphasize DX, respect their time
- **Users**: Focus on outcomes, minimize jargon, show value immediately
- **Stakeholders**: Lead with impact, connect to business goals
- **Technical leaders**: Balance depth with strategic vision

### 2. Structure Creates Clarity
- **Hook**: Grab attention immediately (problem, insight, or promise)
- **Context**: Orient the reader (what, why, who)
- **Journey**: Guide through information logically
- **Resolution**: Deliver on promise, provide clear next steps

### 3. Show, Don't Just Tell
- Concrete examples > abstract descriptions
- Screenshots/diagrams > walls of text
- Live demos > feature lists
- Real outcomes > hypothetical benefits

### 4. Respect Cognitive Load
- Progressive disclosure (simple → complex)
- Scannable structure (headings, bullets, code blocks)
- One concept per section
- Clear signposting ("what you'll learn", "prerequisites")

### 5. Emotional Resonance
- Acknowledge pain points authentically
- Celebrate wins (yours and theirs)
- Build connection through voice and tone
- Create moments of delight

## Anti-Patterns

**Don't:**
- ❌ Use narrative design to obscure technical truth - stories enhance clarity, never replace honesty
- ❌ Prioritize style over substance - narrative serves the content
- ❌ Write for yourself - always audience-first
- ❌ Over-promise in narratives - credibility matters more than hype
- ❌ Ignore structure - storytelling has proven frameworks for good reason

## Getting Started

1. **Identify your artifact**: README? Demo? Blog post?
2. **Select appropriate skill**: Use router above
3. **Understand your audience**: Who reads/watches this?
4. **Apply narrative framework**: Follow skill's structure
5. **Iterate**: Test with real users, refine based on feedback
